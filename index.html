vocabulary
• Animesexual — A term popularized in Western anime fandoms to categorize an attraction to anime characters as a form of sexuality, aligning it with the LGBTQ+ spectrum. The more accurate Japanese term is 'Nijikon.'
• Cunny — A combination of 'cute' and 'funny,' this term is subtly used within certain anime circles to refer to young female characters.
• Cunnysseur — A term that is alternatively used to describe individuals who are fans of young female anime characters, known as Lolicons.
• Double Agent — An individual who strategically aligns with two opposing groups to relay information between them.
• Fictosexuality — A contentious term used to define sexual attraction to fictional characters.
• Gaslighting — A psychological manipulation tactic where factual information is twisted to make the victim doubt their own perception of reality.
• Groupthink — A phenomenon where a group's desire for unanimity leads to irrational decision-making, suppressing dissenting opinions.
• Kaomoji — A Japanese-style emoticon, composed vertically using keyboard characters.
• Kusogaki — A Japanese slang term, translating to 'spoiled brat,' used in non-sexual contexts.
• Larping — Short for 'Live Action Role Playing.' In internet vernacular, it refers to pretending or fabricating situations, often with deceitful intentions.
• Mesugaki — A term for a bold, mischievous young girl character in anime, often depicted as teasing adult men and typically seen in sexualized contexts.
• Necrobumping — The act of reviving a long-inactive forum thread by posting a new message to bring it to prominence.
• Nijikon — A Japanese term expressing an emotional or aesthetic preference for two-dimensional anime, manga, and light novel characters over real people.
• OPSEC — Abbreviation for 'Operational Security,' a process to safeguard sensitive information from unauthorized access.
• OSINT — Stands for 'Open Source Intelligence,' the practice of collecting and analyzing publicly available data for intelligence, distinct from unethical practices such as doxxing.
• Overton Window — The range of ideas accepted in public discourse, particularly relevant in political contexts.
• Persecution Complex — An irrational belief of being persecuted despite lack of evidence.
• Plapping — A term from anime fan culture, referring to a specific action often depicted humorously.
• Proship — A term in fandom communities for individuals who respect various fictional pairings, regardless of personal feelings.
• Schediaphilia — A paraphilia involving sexual attraction to animated or cartoon characters.
• Schizo — A derogatory abbreviation for schizophrenia, misused to label unconventional behavior or beliefs.
• Shilling — Covertly promoting a product or service while posing as an impartial party.
• Social Engineering — Techniques used to manipulate social dynamics for personal gain.
• Sugarcoat — Using euphemistic language to make unpalatable truths seem more acceptable.
• Tech-Illiterate — A term for someone with limited understanding and skill in technology.
• Toonophilia — Another term for sexual attraction to animated characters.
• Tranny — An offensive and outdated term historically used for transgender individuals.
• Troon — A derogatory portmanteau combining 'trans' and 'goon,' used to disparage transgender people.
• Ultracrepidarian — Someone who speaks on topics beyond their knowledge, from the Latin 'ultra' and 'crepidam.'
• YWNBAW — Abbreviation for 'You Will Never Be A Woman,' a derogatory phrase used against transgender individuals.
• 😭 — Used in certain anime contexts to signify the perceived attractiveness of a young female character.

メンヘラ (Menhera) ✧(>o<)ﾉ✧
Menhera, Japanese net slang, refers to peeps who might be battling mental health issues. Originally a buzzword on 2Channel's Menherer (now Menhera) board, it started as "Menhel" from "mental health". (･ω<)☆ It's a broad term, not pinned to any specific illness but full of nuanced meanings. While Menhera aimed to dodge discrimination, it's still wrapped in stigma. These individuals can be unpredictable, showing behaviors like stalking, epic tantrums, craving attention, loyalty tests, and even self-harm threats. (⊙_☉) By the late 2010s, Menhera morphed into a fashion statement through 'Yami Kawaii' substyle, boldly challenging Japan's view on mental health. Think pastel med accessories, syringes, pins with nooses + pills, and vent art on oversized clothes! Super kawaii yet deep. (✿◠‿◠) "Wrist cut warrior Menherachan/メンヘラチャン" by Bisuko Ezaki, began as vent art and became a Yami Kawaii icon! But, plot twist! In 2019, Ezaki trademarked 'Menhera,' causing a stir. Now, it's often seen by Western Instagrammers as just a 'sickly cute aesthetic.' （╹◡╹） Dating a Menhera? It's like, super intense, and breaking up? That's a whole new level of drama! (⊙﹏⊙✿)
